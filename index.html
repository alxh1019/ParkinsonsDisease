Indicators of Parkinson's Disease

Overall Introduction

Parkinson's disease affects millions worldwide, often going undetected for years before visible motor symptoms appear. In this webpage, data of motor indicators of Parkinson's disease via existing studies compile data visualizations in hopes to guide users in understanding this disease.

Data Introduction

This project integrates multiple datasets to provide a comprehensive view of the indicators of Parkinson's Disease. Below is an overview of the data sources used in our analysis.

1. Voice Inflections of Parkinson's Patients

Source: UC Irvine Machine Learning Repository

Description: Average / Minimum / Maximum Vocal Fundamental Frequency and Percentage of Jitters / Shimmers in Healthy and Parkinson's Patients.

  • Size: 195 Voice Recordings
  • Relevant Variables: MDVP:Fo(Hz), MDVP:Fhi(Hz), MDVP:Flo(Hz), MDVP:Jitter, MDVP:Shimmer, status

2. Smartwatch Compiled Data of Parkinson's Patients with Questionnaire

Source: PADS - Parkinsons Disease Smartwatch dataset

Description: A Questionnaire

  • Size: 469 Individuals
  • Relevant Variables: The following questions asked to each individual in True / False format
    01 - Dribbling of saliva during the daytime
    02 - Loss or change in your ability to taste or smell
    03 - Difficulty swallowing food or drink or problems with choking
    04 - Vomiting or feelings of sickness (nausea)
    05 - Constipation (less than 3 bowel movements a week) or having to strain to pass a stool (faeces)
    06 - Bowel (fecal) incontinence
    07 - Feeling that your bowel emptying is incomplete after having been to the toilet
    08 - A sense of urgency to pass urine makes you rush to the toilet
    09 - Getting up regularly at night to pass urine
    10 - Unexplained pains (not due to known conditions such as arthritis)
    11 - Unexplained change in weight (not due to change in diet)
    12 - Problems remembering things that have happened recently or forgetting to do things
    13 - Loss of interest in what is happening around you or doing things
    14 - Seeing or hearing things that you know or are told are not there
    15 - Difficulty concentrating or staying focussed
    16 - "Feeling sad, low or blue"
    17 - "Feeling anxious, frightened or panicky"
    18 - Feeling less interested in sex or more interested in sex
    19 - Finding it difficult to have sex when you try
    20 - "Feeling light headed, dizzy or weak standing from sitting or lying"
    21 - Falling
    22 - "Finding it difficult to stay awake during activities such as working, driving or eating"
    23 - Difficulty getting to sleep at night or staying asleep at night
    24 - "Intense, vivid dreams or frightening dreams"
    25 - Talking or moving about in your sleep as if you are acting out a dream
    26 - "Unpleasant sensations in your legs at night or while resting, and a feeling that you need to move"
    27 - Swelling of your legs
    28 - Excessive sweating
    29 - Double vision
    30 - Believing things are happening to you that other people say are not true
